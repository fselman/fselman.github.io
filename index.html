I am an astronomer working at Paranal observatory as instrument scientist for the MUSE integral field spectrograph, and giving general observatory support. In the past I have been the instrument scientist for VIMOS, OmegaCAM, HAWKI, and the Wide Field Imager at La Silla.

My main research interest have been in the study of the massive stellar population spectrum of masses at birth, the IMF, in different environments ranging from the field population in the LMC around the 30 Doradus superassociation to the Arches cluster near the center of the Milky Way galaxy. I have also done some work on the scale-free character of the mass function and have convinced myself that the observations are consistent with a purely random sampling of a universal IMF. Other research interest have ranged from the study of the intergalactic light in clusters of galaxies to the analysis of the ionized gas in the tail of ram stripped galaxies infalling in clusters of galaxies.

Currently I am interested in exploring ways to extract information from MUSE datacubes, and on the challenge of visualizing these observations.
